Question

Onkel Hans is enjoying a libration in the revolving restaurant in the CN tower. The restaurant is circular as shown, with a radius \(R_1=7\text{ m}\), rotating with a period of \(1\) rotation clockwise every \(5\) minutes. In order to increase the dizzying effect of the experience, Hans rigs his circular table (\(R_2=1\text{ m}\)) to also rotate, counterclocwise, with a period of one rotation every \(4\) minutes, relative to the floor of the restaurant, such that the center of Hans\ table is \(5\text{ m}\) away. If he begins his ride seated at the edge of his table, at position \((0,6)\) at time \(t=0\) as shown, what's his speed, relative to the origin, after \(3\) minutes have passed? Answer in \(\text{m/s}\).
\(\textbf{(A) } 0.105 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 0.650 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 0.713 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 0.863 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 0.910\)

Solution 1

Let's first see if we can place an upper bound on the speed of the person. The speed of the center of the table is given by: $$v = r\omega = (5)\left(\frac{2\pi}{300}\right) = 0.1047$$ and the speed of the person relative to the table is $$v = r\omega = (1)\left(\frac{2\pi}{240}\right) = 0.0262$$ The maximum speed of the person relative to the origin is when these two velocities point in the same direcrtion in which case: \begin{align*} v_\text{person relative origin} &= v_\text{person relative table center} + v_\text{table center relative origin} \\ &= 0.0262+0.1047 \\ &=0.1309 \end{align*} The only value that is below this maximum speed is \(\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 0.105}\)
