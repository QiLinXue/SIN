Question

A rebellious faction of Newfoundlanders is determined to secede from Confederation. They fire a flaming bag of moose droppings (mass \(20 \text{ kg}\)) across the Gulf of St. Lawrence toward the mainland at an angle of \(60\) degrees with a muzzle velocity of \(100 \text{ m/s}\). Immediately after the cannon fires, the Royal Newfoundland Constabulary seizes it back from the rebels. Hoping to douse the flames, they load the cannon with a water balloon (mass \(8\text{ kg}\)) and change the launch angle to \(30\) degrees. How long after the first projectile was fired must the Constabulary launch the balloon such that they collide mid-air? The muzzle velocity of the water is \(80 \text{ m/s}\). Answer in seconds.
\(\textbf{(A) } 6.84 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 24.6 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 67.1 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 241 \qquad\textbf{(E) } \text{The moose doo unavoidably hits the ground.}\)

Solution 1

Let us attempt to visualize the physical trajectories \(y(x)\) of both objects by using standard kinematic equations and breaking up the motion into its vertical and horizontal components.

For the moose droppings, the position as a function of time can be represented as: \begin{align*} y &= 100\sin(60)t - 4.9t^2 \\ x &= 100\cos(60)t \end{align*} Rearranging the second equation as \(t=\frac{100\cos(60)}{x}\) and substituting the expression for \(t\) into the first equation gives: $$y=\tan(60)x - \frac{4.9x^2}{100^2\cos^2(60)} \approx 1.7x - 0.002x^2$$ Using a similar process, we can determine the equation of the physical trajectory for the water balloon. As before, the position as a function of time is represented as: \begin{align*} y &= 80\sin(30)t - 4.9t^2 \\ x &= 80\cos(30)t \end{align*} Rearranging the second equation as \(t=\frac{80\cos(30)}{x}\) and substituting the expression for \(t\) into the first equation gives: $$y=\tan(30)x - \frac{4.9x^2}{80^2\cos^2(30)} \approx 0.58x - 0.001x^2$$ Setting these two expressions for the position equal to each other gives the intersection point at which the two objects collide. Solving \(1.7x - 0.002x^2 = 0.58x - 0.001x^2\) gives \((x,y)=(1120,-605)\). Since the physical paths do not intersect above the x-axis, then it is impossible for them to collide. Thus, \(\boxed{\textbf{(E) }\text{the moose doo unavoidably hits the ground}}\)

Solution 2

Perhaps we can solve this problem by eliminating some answer choices. When do the moose droppings hit the floor? The height of the moose droppings is given by the equation $$y=100\sin(60)t-4.9t^2 \approx 86.6t-4.9t^2$$ Setting this to zero gives \(t=17.67\text{ sec}\). The only options this leaves is \(\textbf{(A) } 6.84\) and \(\textbf{(E) }\text{the moose doo unavoidably hits the ground}\). Let us try to test out option \(\textbf{(A)}\) by hand.

The equations of motion of the two objects in the x-direction are given below: \begin{align*} x_\text{moose droppings} &= 100\cos(60)t \\ x_\text{water balloon} &= 80\cos(30)(t-6.84) \\ \end{align*} Setting them equal gives: \begin{align*} 100\cos(60)t &= 80\cos(30)(t-6.84) \\ 50t &= 69.28t - 473.8752 \\ t &= 24.58 \end{align*} However, we have already established that \(t \le 17.67\) so option \(\textbf{(A)}\) is wrong. By process of elimination \(\boxed{\textbf{(E) }\text{the moose doo unavoidably hits the ground}}\).
