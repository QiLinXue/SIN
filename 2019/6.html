Question

A Canada goose braves the winter in an open pond of water at the University of Waterloo. Come spring her eggs hatch, and she flies East \(200 \text{ km}\) where she discovers Quebec's strategic reserve of poutine. Gravving a bill full of delicious curds, she then flies back West to feed her goslings. The goose's speed relative to the air is \(8\text{ m/s}\). If the wind is blowing from the East and the goose's average speed for the round trip is \(7.5 \text{ m/s}\), what is the speed of the wind relative to the ground? Answer in m/s
\(\textbf{(A) } 1.0 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 2.0 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 3.5 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 7.5 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 8.0 \)

Solution

Let \(x\) be the speed of the wind relative to the ground. Therefore, on the way to Quebec and on the way back, respectively: $$(8+x)t_1 = 200,000$$ $$(8-x)t_2 = 200,000$$ And the average speed is \(v_\text{avg}=\frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t} = \frac{400,000}{t_1+t_2} = 7.5\). We have three unknowns and three equations. Combining them together gives: \begin{align*} t_1 + t_2 &= 53333.33 \\ \frac{200,000}{8+x} + \frac{200,000}{8-x} &= 53333.33 \\ 200,000(8-x) + 200,000(8+x) &= 53333.33(8-x)(8+x) \\ 200,000(8-x+8+x) &= 53333.33(64-x^2) \\ 3,200,000 &= 3,413,333.12 - 5333.3x^2 \\ 213333.12 &= 5333.3x^2 \\ x &= 2 \end{align*} Thus, the answer is \(\boxed{\textbf{(B) } 2.0}\)
